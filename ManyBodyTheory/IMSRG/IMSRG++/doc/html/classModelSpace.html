Detailed Description

Model space from emax, reference(hole) and valence

orbit index = (2*n+l)*(2*n+l+3) + 1-j2 + (tz2+1)/2 . Thus 0 = "p0s1", 1 = "n0s1", 2= "p0p3", 3="n0p3" etc. proton has tz2=-1 , neutron is +1.

◆ PreCalculateSixJ()

void ModelSpace::PreCalculateSixJ ( )

Loop over all the 6j symbols that we expect to encounter, and store them in a hash table. Calculate all symbols

\[ \begin{Bmatrix} ja & jb & J1 jc & jd & J2 \end{Bmatrix} \]

and

\[ \begin{Bmatrix} J1 & J2 & J3 ja & jb & jc \end{Bmatrix} \]

where ja,jb,jc are half-integer and J1,J2,J3 are integer. ja,jb,jc run from 1/2 to emax+1/2, while jd runs higher since the 3N recoupling requires it to go up to e(emax+1/2). I haven't yet bothered using the symmetry properties of the 6j symbol.
